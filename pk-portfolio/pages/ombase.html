Invision Tour

It’s easy to say that when a client approaches you with a new problem you should gather all data possible before providing a solution. In the real world, designers are often left with half the picture. Its almost always the case of trying to identify not just what the customer wants, but what will best serve their needs. Far too often we build tools to solve a problem only to have gaps now created by the new work flow.


Checking In

OMBase is a scheduling and client management system used and developed by YourYoga. Its core functionality is logging the status of client’s memberships and checking them into classes. After visiting their space it became clear that the workflow required a plethora of tools for their business needs.

Students enter the space via a long staircase. At the top of the staircase is a small table with the current session’s instructor. Each client writes their name and email on a clipboard before they put their personal belongings into a cubby-hole. The instructor then reviews the clipboard and enters the persons name into the check-in screen.


If the client is new or needs to re-up their subscription, the instructor needs to utilize square, a separate app for paying with a credit card. Any other means of payment is manually entered into a google spreadsheet. Most of the time the instructor is sharing dialogue with the customer to keep them entertained as they enter the space. They juggle their time at desk with helping students get set up, often having to wait until after class to re-enter names they missed via the clipboard.


This workflow leads to a lack of connection between the instructor and students as they enter the space. What is supposed to feel like a tranquil, welcoming environment feels cluttered and chaotic. By shifting the roles of instructor and student, OMBase could better facilitate the welcome.

Change the Perspective

Ask students to check-in online prior to arriving at the studio or via an iPad that sits at the front table. With a subtle change to the check-in process, YourYoga instructors would have more time to focus on the thing that matters most, their students. Building that report with students and improving their experience from entrance to exit. A self check-in page could be developed that allows each student the flexibility to check-in on their way to class or when they arrive. The instructors would negotiate issues and edge-cases via their own device or by switching screens on the iPad.


To improve the effectiveness of instructor’s ability to resolve problems, I established a stronger hierarchy and alignment to common mental models. Many button and interactive elements on OMBase hide behind a click. I brought those elements to the forefront in a way to call attention to an instructor’s most used functions. This creates a more shallow learning curve and reduces the potential for unintentional errors.

Wrap Up

This new workflow affects not just the instructor’s experience but also the students’. Shifting the role of instructor from greeter to facilitator. Each interaction with a business is an opportunity to gain loyalty or lose trust. These small changes will close the gap between the necessary tasks and a successful check-in process.
